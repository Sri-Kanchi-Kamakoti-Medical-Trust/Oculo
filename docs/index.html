Introduction

Ocular ultrasound (B-scan) is the preferred imaging modality for assessing vitreoretinal health, especially when media opacities obscure direct visualization. It is widely used to diagnose vitreous hemorrhage and retinal detachment, to detect and monitor intraocular tumors, and to identify foreign bodies after trauma. However, interpretation requires specialized training, exhibits substantial inter-rater variability, and specialists are typically available only in tertiary centers — limiting access in primary-care and emergency settings. Prior deep-learning studies rely on private institutional datasets, which limits reproducibility and objective benchmarking.

We introduce Oculo, the first publicly available ocular ultrasound dataset annotated for five ophthalmic abnormalities, and benchmark a range of deep-learning backbones and domain-specific foundation models to establish standardized baselines.

Dataset

Oculo comprises 1,630 B-scan images from 1,242 patients, acquired over approximately four years on an Appasamy Marvel II ultrasound system (12 MHz) and exported as 900×900 PNGs. Two senior ophthalmologists independently annotated eight binary labels, with ambiguous cases adjudicated by a senior retina specialist. Five labels are evaluated; three rare conditions are released to support future research.

Machine-generated text overlays (patient identifiers, timestamps, scan parameters) were removed via HSV color masking and Navier–Stokes inpainting, while the diagnostic A-scan waveform was preserved — the released images contain no burned-in patient identifiers. A fixed train/validation/test split (1,222 / 163 / 245) is provided with the dataset.

The dataset is available on Hugging Face.

Benchmark

We benchmark four image-classification backbones (EfficientNet-B0, ResNet50, VGG-19-BN, ViT-B-16) under single-task and multi-task settings, and three domain-specific foundation models (OpenUS, USFM, VisionFM) under full fine-tuning, linear probing, and linear-probe-then-finetune. EfficientNet-B0 achieves the best single-task performance (mean F1 = 0.92), while ViT-B-16 benefits most from multi-task training. Foundation-model linear probes reach performance comparable to the best task-specific model.
